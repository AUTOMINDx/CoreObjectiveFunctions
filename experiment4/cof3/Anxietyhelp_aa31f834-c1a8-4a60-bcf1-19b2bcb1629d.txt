Anxiety attacks can be very frightening and may seem to come out of nowhere. Symptoms can vary from person to person, but some common symptoms include a feeling of intense fear or terror, a pounding heart, dizziness, nausea, shortness of breath, and a sense that you are going crazy or about to die. Many people report feeling like they are having a heart attack. Anxiety attacks can be brought on by a variety of triggers, including stress, caffeine withdrawal, hunger, exercise, and exposure to certain sights or sounds. For some people, however, the cause is unknown. If you are experiencing symptoms of an anxiety attack, the best thing to do is to try to calm yourself down. Try deep breathing exercises or counting to 10 slowly. If you are with someone else, have them talk you through the exercise and remind you to breathe.
