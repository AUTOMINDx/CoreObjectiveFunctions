Good &amp; Gifts: Distinction between and it's social and ecological imprint
Unlike gifts, goods - what is bought and sold in a market - are said to be free from social obligations and refrains. For example, you have no obligations to the grocery store where you buy food, nor to the farmers who produce the food. You may not even recognize the cashier's appearance afterwards. Hornborg and Tsing problematizes this with goods in different ways. Tsing wants to loosen the sharp line between goods and gifts and for Hornborg it is important to highlight the product's social and ecological
imprint. What do they mean?
